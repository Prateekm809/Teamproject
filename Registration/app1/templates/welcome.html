Meaning of "Taekwondo"

The word "Taekwondo" can be broken down as follows:
1.Tae (태): Represents foot techniques, including kicks and strikes with the feet.
2.Kwon (권): Represents hand techniques, including punches and strikes with the hands.
3.Do (도): Means "the way" or "the path." It signifies the martial art's philosophical and moral aspects.

Five Tenets of Taekwondo:

1.Courtesy (예의, Ye-ui):
Definition: Courtesy in Taekwondo refers to showing respect, politeness, and consideration toward others.
Practical Application: Taekwondo practitioners are taught to bow to instructors and fellow students as a sign of respect. This tenet emphasizes the importance of humility and treating others with kindness.

2.Integrity (염치, Yeomchi):
Definition: Integrity means being honest, truthful, and morally upright in all actions and decisions.
Practical Application: Taekwondo students are encouraged to maintain honesty both on and off the training mat. This tenet instills a sense of ethics and moral character.

3.Perseverance (인내, Innai):
Definition: Perseverance involves maintaining determination, resilience, and patience in the face of challenges and obstacles.
Practical Application: Taekwondo teaches students to never give up, even when facing difficulties during training or in life. This tenet fosters mental strength and determination.

4.Self-Control (극기, Geukgi):
Definition: Self-control means having discipline over one's actions, emotions, and impulses.
Practical Application: Taekwondo emphasizes the importance of controlling one's temper and avoiding aggression. Practitioners learn to harness their energy and focus it on productive goals.

5.Indomitable Spirit (백절불굴, Baekjeol Bulgul):
Definition: Indomitable spirit is the unwavering determination to overcome challenges and never be defeated.
Practical Application: Taekwondo encourages a strong will and the belief that no obstacle is insurmountable. This tenet inspires students to face adversity with courage.
